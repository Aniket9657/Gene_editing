# Gondia District – Structured Knowledge Base (RAG Ready)

## 1. Basic Information

Gondia district is located in the eastern part of Maharashtra, India, in the Vidarbha region. It was established in 1999 after being separated from Bhandara district. The administrative headquarters is Gondia city.

* State: Maharashtra
* Region: Vidarbha
* Established: 1999
* Headquarters: Gondia

---

## 2. Geography

Gondia district lies near the borders of Madhya Pradesh and Chhattisgarh. The region consists of plains, forests, and water bodies.

Key features:

* Fertile agricultural land
* Dense forest cover
* Presence of rivers and lakes
* Tropical climate with monsoon rainfall

---

## 3. Economy

The economy is primarily agriculture-based.

Main components:

* Major crop: Rice
* Known as: “Rice City” or “Rice Bowl”
* Agro-based industries: Rice mills
* Secondary activities: Forestry and small-scale industries

---

## 4. Demographics

The population includes both urban and rural communities, along with tribal populations.

Languages spoken:

* Marathi (official)
* Hindi
* Tribal languages

---

## 5. Transport and Connectivity

Gondia district is well connected by rail and road networks.

Important points:

* Major railway station: Gondia Junction
* Road connectivity to nearby cities and states
* Nearest airports: Nagpur and Raipur

---

## 6. Tourism and Environment

The district is known for forests and wildlife tourism.

Major attractions:

* Navegaon National Park (wildlife and birdwatching)
* Nagzira Wildlife Sanctuary (biodiversity and tigers)

Environmental features:

* Rich biodiversity
* Forest reserves
* Eco-tourism potential

---

## 7. Administrative Divisions

Gondia district consists of several tehsils:

* Gondia
* Tirora
* Amgaon
* Deori
* Goregaon
* Sadak Arjuni
* Salekasa
